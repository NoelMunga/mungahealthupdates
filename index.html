Malaria burden across africa



ABSTRACT

Malaria transmission in Sub-Saharan Africa continues to threaten public health despite a gradual decline in disease burden. The region faces challenges due to spatial and temporal variability, contributing to missed prevention opportunities and misdiagnoses.

"the burden of malaria is most felt in the Sub-Saharan region and the statistics show -Noel Munga"
Factors such as population growth, urbanization, and habitat destruction make worse the situation.
Although control strategies and new diagnostic technologies have shown progress, malaria remains a significant threat, with Plasmodium falciparum causing the most severe cases.

RAPID DIAGNOSTIC TEST

In response to the limitations of microscopy for malaria diagnosis, various innovative diagnostic methods have been developed, prompted by the World Health Organization's recognition of the need for quick, accurate, and affordable tests. Rapid Diagnostic Tests (RDTs) have gained popularity due to their simplicity and accessibility, particularly in rural areas where high-quality microscopy may not be available. Over 200 RDT products are currently in use globally, all utilizing the same three antigens: Plasmodium lactate dehydrogenase (pLDH), Plasmodium lactate-rich protein 2 (PfHRP-2), and Plasmodium aldolase (pALDO). These tests function by detecting malaria antigens in blood samples, with results displayed as lines on a strip. While RDTs are effective for diagnosing P. falciparum malaria, a new method has been developed for identifying P. vivax and P. knowlesi malaria. Despite variations in sensitivity and specificity observed among different reports, RDTs generally demonstrate good performance. However, they are increasingly being used alongside other methods to validate results, detect infections, and monitor disease progression. RDTs have facilitated malaria diagnosis in both endemic and non-endemic areas, with ongoing efforts to enhance their sensitivity, potentially shortening the time required for treating imported malaria cases.
